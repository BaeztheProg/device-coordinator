AP Exam Preparation

This course is strategically designed to build the skills and knowledge you need to succeed on the AP Computer Science A exam.

Core Focus: Java & Problem Solving

The entire course is taught in Java, the official language of the exam. You'll master essential concepts like object-oriented programming (OOP) and spend significant time on practical problem-solving and algorithm development, which are critical skills for the free-response questions on the exam.

Course Content Progression

Java Coding Fundamentals

Variables, data types, objects, and control structures.

Iteration and Classes

Writing your own classes, loops, and understanding program logic.

Arrays & ArrayLists

Managing and manipulating large sets of data.

Inheritance and Recursion

Advanced OOP concepts and recursive problem-solving.

Key Course Policies

Please familiarize yourself with these important classroom and school-wide policies.

Late Work and Absences

  • Late Work: May be accepted for partial credit at the instructor's discretion. All late submissions must be turned in before the end of the current unit of study.
  • Missed Quizzes: There are no make-ups for missed quizzes. The grade will be prorated based on your score on the corresponding unit test.
  • Missed Tests : You are responsible for scheduling a make-up with the teacher. It must be completed within the number of days you were absent.

F.I.R.E. & Ethical Actions

Emerald High School's F.I.R.E. motto guides our behavior. The "E" for Ethical Actions is critical in this course.

  • Lab Use: Your higher-level access on lab computers is a privilege. Do not alter software, download unauthorized files, attempt to hack, or damage any equipment.
  • Academic Honesty: All work submitted must be your own. Plagiarism or any form of cheating will result in a zero on the assignment and will be reported to the administration.

Ethical Use of Artificial Intelligence

The academic honesty policy explicitly prohibits the use of generative AI tools (like ChatGPT, Copilot, etc.) to complete assignments or write code for you, unless the teacher gives specific, written permission for a particular task. Using AI without authorization is considered a violation of academic integrity and will be treated as such. The goal is to ensure that the work you submit reflects your own learning and problem-solving abilities.
